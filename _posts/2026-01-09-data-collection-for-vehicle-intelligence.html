While the 6 AM automation solves the immediate charging need, I also want the system to learn which vehicle is plugged in without relying on proprietary car APIs. That means turning raw charging telemetry into labeled data.

Data Collection Loop

Session monitoring: A 10-minute poll detects when a new session starts.
High-resolution capture: Once a session is detected, collect 30 samples at 10-second intervals (5 minutes total).
Store + map: Save the samples and map them to the session ID in data/sessions and data/session_vehicle_map.json.

Why 5 minutes? Early charging behavior captures distinct power ramps that differ between vehicles.

Labeling Workflow

  • Load recent sessions in the labeling UI (Week 5 deliverable).
  • Assign vehicle labels (Equinox vs. Volvo) using session notes.
  • Persist labels to session_vehicle_map.json for training.

Future ML Direction

  • Feature extraction: Power slope, time-to-stabilize, taper patterns.
  • Model: Start with a lightweight classifier (logistic regression / random forest) to avoid overfitting.
  • Inference: When a session starts, classify within the first 2–3 minutes to tag the vehicle automatically.
The goal is privacy-preserving intelligence: no VIN scraping, no OEM APIs—just power signatures.

Why This Matters

Once the system knows which car is present, it can tailor behavior: adjust the 6 AM extension duration, prioritize the vehicle with the lower state of charge, or send vehicle-specific alerts. And it can do it without tapping into manufacturer ecosystems.

What's Next

  • Collect more labeled sessions across seasons (winter vs. summer charging curves).
  • Add a small evaluation set to measure classifier accuracy before automating decisions.
  • Keep the manual override path clear—ML suggestions should be assistive, not authoritative.
